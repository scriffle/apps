Harmonic Solids

Chords and notes are physical objects whose shape is the music theory — not a decoration of it.

One model drives everything you see and hear. Roughness — sensory dissonance computed from the Plomp–Levelt curve across six sawtooth harmonics per note — decides how things fit: consonant notes seat flush, tensions seat partway with an honest gap, and semitone clashes refuse and strain. Hue comes from the pitch-class colour wheel, so transposing just rotates colour. Chords float in a voice-leading lattice where physical distance is the number of semitones the voices move. And the synth plays the exact spectrum the geometry was measured from, so what you see and what you hear can never disagree.

Honest about its limits: sensory roughness is only part of consonance — culture and learning matter too, and this model doesn't capture them. The parallel-5ths penalty in the explorer is a Western convention, not physics.
