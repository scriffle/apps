One course, three passes

Most textbooks make you read everything at full depth, once. This book asks you to read the course three times at three depths — and tells you exactly when each pass earns its keep.

How the passes connect

Every topic is linked vertically. From a Pass 1 story you can drop into the Pass 2 core; from any Pass 2 section you can climb up to the story or down to the dot-point detail. Links that look like this carry you between depths:

↑ Pass 1 · the story ● Pass 2 · the core ↓ Pass 3 · the detail

Chevron bars like the one below expand and collapse. Headings stay visible; detail appears only when you ask for it. Your open sections are remembered between visits, and printing a page expands everything automatically.

What's in this edition

Five areas of study are built at full depth: Unit 3, Area of Study 1 — How do physicists explain motion in two dimensions? (projectile, circular and satellite motion; momentum, impulse and energy), Unit 3, Area of Study 2 — How do things move without contact? (gravitational, electric and magnetic fields; satellites; DC motors; particle accelerators), Unit 3, Area of Study 3 — How are fields used in electricity generation? (electromagnetic induction; generators and alternators; transformers and transmission), Unit 4, Area of Study 1 — How has understanding about the physical world changed? (light as wave and particle; matter waves; spectra; special relativity; mass–energy), and Unit 4, Area of Study 2 — How is scientific inquiry used to investigate fields, motion or light? (designing, measuring, analysing and reporting a practical investigation). Figure and simulation slots are specified but not yet illustrated.
