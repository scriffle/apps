LED Screen Simulator

From quantum physics to the screen in your pocket

Every image on every screen is built from tiny red, green, and blue lights at different intensities. This simulator lets you explore that idea across four zoom levels:

●Pixel

Zoom levels

  • Physics — Three PN junction LEDs with electron flow simulations. Each drives one colour channel (R/G/B).
  • Pixel — A single tri-colour pixel with three subpixel emitters mixing into one colour.
  • Array — A grid of pixels forming a visible image, with individual subpixels discernible.
  • Phone — A phone screen showing the full image. Use the magnifying glass to zoom into the pixel array.

Controls

  • Three voltage sliders control the Red, Green, and Blue LED brightness (0–255)
  • Use the bottom navigation bar or keys 1–4 to switch zoom levels
  • ← → arrow keys step between views
This simulation is for pedagogical purposes only. It uses simplified models to illustrate how LED screens produce colour from subpixel light emission. Particle counts, speeds, and proportions are not to scale. Real displays use more complex driver circuits and millions of pixels.
