Question 1: Tug of War - Net Force

Problem: Two men are having a tug of war. One man pulls with 200 N to the left, the other pulls with 150 N to the right. Find the net force.

Solution:

Define positive direction as right, negative as left.

Forces: Left = -200 N, Right = +150 N
Net Force = -200 N + 150 N = -50 N

The negative sign indicates the direction is to the left.

Answer: D) 50 N left

Question 2: Scalar vs Vector Quantities

Problem: Which of the following is NOT a scalar quantity?

  • A) mass
  • B) time
  • C) acceleration
  • D) speed

Solution:

Scalar quantities have magnitude only:

  • Mass - scalar (only magnitude, e.g., 5 kg)
  • Time - scalar (only magnitude, e.g., 10 seconds)
  • Speed - scalar (only magnitude, e.g., 20 m/s)

Vector quantities have both magnitude and direction:

  • Acceleration - vector (magnitude and direction, e.g., 5 m/s² downward)
Answer: C) acceleration

Question 3: Tension Force Components

Problem: A towing cable has tension of 20,000 N at 15° to the horizontal. Find the horizontal component.

Solution:

Horizontal component = T × cos(θ)
= 20,000 × cos(15°)
= 20,000 × 0.9659
= 19,318 N ≈ 19,320 N
Answer: B) 19,320 N

Question 4: Change in Velocity

Problem: A basketball hits the ground with velocity 7.80 m/s down and rebounds with 5.30 m/s up. Find the change in velocity.

Solution:

Define up as positive, down as negative:

Initial velocity (v₁) = -7.80 m/s (down)
Final velocity (v₂) = +5.30 m/s (up)
Change in velocity (Δv) = v₂ - v₁
Δv = 5.30 - (-7.80) = 5.30 + 7.80 = 13.10 m/s

Positive result means the change is upward.

Answer: A) 13.1 m/s up

  • Ball comes down: distance continues to increase
  • Graph should never decrease or go negative

Question 7: Relative Motion

Problem: Driver drops sunglasses from car moving at 72 km/h. Describe the motion as seen by driver and pedestrians.

Solution:

From driver's perspective: The sunglasses maintain the car's horizontal velocity, so they appear to fall straight down.

From pedestrians' perspective: The sunglasses have both horizontal (72 km/h) and vertical (gravity) motion, following a parabolic path.

Answer: B) The sunglasses will move vertically down and not move horizontally, as seen by the driver

Question 8: Average Acceleration from Graph

Problem: From the velocity-time graph, calculate average acceleration between points A and B.

Solution:

From the graph:

At point A (t = 6s): v = 23 m/s
At point B (t = 9s): v = 11 m/s
Average acceleration = Δv/Δt = (11 - 23)/(9 - 6)
= -12/3 = -4.0 m/s²
Answer: D) -4.00 m/s²

Question 9: Distance from Velocity-Time Graph

Problem: Calculate distance between points A and B using the velocity-time graph.

Solution:

Distance = Area under velocity-time graph

Using trapezoid formula for area between t=6s and t=9s:

Area = ½ × (v₁ + v₂) × Δt
= ½ × (23 + 11) × 3
= ½ × 34 × 3
= 51 m

Closest answer is 48 m (accounting for graph reading precision)

Answer: C) 48 m

Question 11: Falling Objects - Skydiver and Pen

Problem: Skydiver at constant 5 m/s down, drops pen from 100m height.

Part (a): Speed of pen when it hits ground

Using: v² = u² + 2as
u = 5 m/s (initial velocity, same as skydiver)
a = 9.8 m/s² (gravity)
s = 100 m
v² = 5² + 2(9.8)(100) = 25 + 1960 = 1985
v = √1985 = 44.6 m/s

Part (b): Time for pen to hit ground

Using: s = ut + ½at²
100 = 5t + ½(9.8)t²
4.9t² + 5t - 100 = 0
Using quadratic formula: t = 4.04 s

Part (c): Time for skydiver after pen lands

Skydiver's time = Distance/Speed = 100/5 = 20 s
Time after pen lands = 20 - 4.04 = 15.96 s

Question 14: Water Rocket Forces

Problem: Water rocket with thrust 1.5 N up, weight 0.5 N down. After 0.433s, velocity is 8.55 m/s up at height 4.33m.

Part (a): Is force a vector or scalar?

Force is a vector quantity because it has both magnitude and direction.

Justification: Forces can be added using vector addition, they have direction (up/down), and they follow vector laws.

Part (b): Net force on rocket

Thrust (up) = +1.5 N
Weight (down) = -0.5 N
Net force = 1.5 - 0.5 = 1.0 N upward

Part (c): Time to reach maximum height

After fuel runs out, only gravity acts:

Initial velocity = 8.55 m/s (up)
Acceleration = -9.8 m/s² (gravity)
At max height, v = 0
Using: v = u + at
0 = 8.55 + (-9.8)t
t = 8.55/9.8 = 0.872 s

Question 15: Car Deceleration

Problem: Car slows from 20 m/s to 11 m/s north over 88.7 m.

Part (a): Change in velocity

Initial velocity = 20 m/s north
Final velocity = 11 m/s north
Δv = v₂ - v₁ = 11 - 20 = -9 m/s
Direction: 9 m/s south (opposite to motion)
Change in velocity = 9 m/s south

Part (b): Time of braking

Using: s = (u + v)t/2
88.7 = (20 + 11)t/2
88.7 = 31t/2
t = 2 × 88.7/31 = 5.72 s

Part (c): Acceleration

a = Δv/t = -9/5.72 = -1.57 m/s²
Direction: 1.57 m/s² south

Question 16: Charged Ball Equilibrium

Problem: Ball suspended by string with 200 N horizontal force and 550 N weight.

Solution:

At equilibrium, the string tension balances both forces:

Horizontal component: T sin(θ) = 200 N
Vertical component: T cos(θ) = 550 N
Dividing: tan(θ) = 200/550 = 0.364
θ = arctan(0.364) = 20.0°
Angle = 20.0°

Question 17: Car Acceleration from Rest

Problem: Car accelerates from rest to 99 km/h in 7.91 s.

Part (a): Magnitude of acceleration

Convert: 99 km/h = 99 × (1000/3600) = 27.5 m/s
Initial velocity (u) = 0 m/s
Final velocity (v) = 27.5 m/s
Time (t) = 7.91 s
a = (v - u)/t = 27.5/7.91 = 3.48 m/s²

Part (b): Distance traveled

Using: s = ut + ½at²
s = 0 + ½(3.48)(7.91)²
s = ½(3.48)(62.57)
s = 108.8 m

Question 18: Coin Tossed Into Well

Problem: Coin flicked up at 3.75 m/s from 5 m above water surface.

Part (a): Maximum height above water

At max height, v = 0
Using: v² = u² - 2gh
0 = (3.75)² - 2(9.8)h
h = 14.06/19.6 = 0.72 m
Total height above water = 5 + 0.72 = 5.72 m

Part (b): Speed when hitting water

Total fall distance = 5.72 m
Using: v² = u² + 2gs
v² = 0² + 2(9.8)(5.72)
v² = 112.1
v = 10.6 m/s

Part (c): Total flight time

Time up: t₁ = u/g = 3.75/9.8 = 0.383 s
Time down from max height:
Using: s = ½gt²
5.72 = ½(9.8)t₂²
t₂ = 1.08 s
Total time = 0.383 + 1.08 = 1.46 s

Question 19: Car and Truck Motion

Problem: Analyze motion from velocity-time graph where car starts after truck passes.

Part (a): Describe the car's motion

  • 0-10s: Car accelerates uniformly from 0 to 25 m/s
  • 10-50s: Car maintains constant velocity of 25 m/s
  • Truck maintains constant 20 m/s throughout

Part (b): Average acceleration in first 10s

a = Δv/Δt = (25 - 0)/(10 - 0) = 2.5 m/s²

Part (c): When car overtakes truck

Car overtakes when distances are equal:

Truck distance: s = 20t
Car distance (0-10s): s₁ = ½(2.5)(10)² = 125 m
Car distance (after 10s): s₂ = 125 + 25(t-10)

When equal: 20t = 125 + 25(t-10)
20t = 125 + 25t - 250
-5t = -125
t = 25 seconds

Question 20: Hawk Catching Food

Problem: Food dropped from balloon, hawk starts 0.730s later at 20 m/s, then free falls.

Part (a): Time when food is caught

Food motion: s_food = ½gt² (from rest)
Hawk motion: starts at t = 0.730s with v = 20 m/s

Food position at time t: s_f = ½g(t)²
Hawk position: s_h = 20(t-0.730) + ½g(t-0.730)²

When caught: s_f = s_h
½g(t)² = 20(t-0.730) + ½g(t-0.730)²

Solving: t = 1.53 seconds

Part (b): Distance food travels

s = ½gt² = ½(9.8)(1.53)²
s = 11.5 m
