When Your Powerhouse is Not Powerhousing

A Year 8 Guide to Mitochondrial Replacement Therapy

Remember learning about cells? Every cell in your body has tiny structures called mitochondria - they're like miniature power stations that give your cells energy to work.

Real mitochondria seen through an electron microscope

Fun Fact: You have about 37 trillion cells in your body, and most of them contain hundreds or even thousands of mitochondria.

But what happens when these powerhouses stop working properly? That's what we're going to explore today, along with an amazing scientific breakthrough that's helping families in the UK.

When Powerhouses Break Down

What Goes Wrong?

Sometimes babies are born with faulty mitochondria due to mistakes in mitochondrial DNA.

Nuclear DNA: Main instructions (99.9%)
Mitochondrial DNA: Just for mitochondria (0.1%)

Key fact: You only inherit mitochondrial DNA from your mum because the egg cell provides all the mitochondria.

Quick Question:

Can fathers pass on mitochondrial diseases?

No - only mothers can pass them on through the egg cell.

The Amazing Solution

Scientists in the UK developed Mitochondrial Replacement Therapy (MRT) - like swapping a broken battery for a working one.

How mitochondrial replacement therapy works

How It Works:

1 Take the Good: Remove nuclear DNA from mother's egg
2 Find a Helper: Get egg from donor with healthy mitochondria
3 Make the Switch: Replace donor's nuclear DNA with mother's
4 Create Life: Fertilize with father's sperm

DNA Breakdown:

50% Nuclear DNA from Mum

50% Nuclear DNA from Dad

0.1% Mitochondrial DNA from Donor

Why This Matters

UK First The UK was the first country to allow this in 2015. First UK babies were born in 2023.

Who Does This Help?

Families where the mother carries faulty mitochondrial DNA. Without treatment, children might have:

  • Severe muscle weakness
  • Heart problems
  • Brain disorders
  • Vision or hearing loss

Breaking the Chain Forever

The donor contributes only 0.1% of DNA - like one drop of milk in a glass of orange juice.

The amazing part: If the baby is a girl, she'll pass on healthy mitochondria to her children, breaking the disease chain for all future generations.
