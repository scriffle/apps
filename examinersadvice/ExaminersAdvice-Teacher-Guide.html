Teacher's Guide

ExaminersAdvice

See like the examiner sees.

A companion to the ExaminersAdvice app. It explains how the app works, where the questions come from, how students send you their results, and why the app uses no accounts and stores no data that you need to manage.

Try the app ↓ Why no accounts?

1 What it is

A study tool customised for every VCE study and built from the current examiners' reports. The advice in those reports is presented to students as short true/false quizzes, covering the errors and misconceptions that examiners report each year.

Each point of advice is presented twice, as two separate questions on the same concept. A concept is recorded as mastered only when both of its questions are answered correctly. This paired format confirms genuine understanding rather than a single fortunate guess.

📋

From the reports

Every prompt is a point of advice taken from an examiners' report, not generic revision.

✓✓

Paired questions

Two questions per concept. Mastered only when both are correct.

🔗

No setup or accounts

No installation and no marking. You send a link, and students begin.

3 How it works

The interface is three tabs and two selectors. Open any row for detail, and try each one in the live app above.

Choosing a study area & subjectThe two dropdowns at the top

Students select a study area (for example, Science) and then a subject (for example, Physics). The optional Student name field is used only to label exports and emails. It is not sent anywhere on its own.

The Questions tabTrue / false, with instant feedback

Each prompt is answered True or False. The student then sees the explanation and a short study tip drawn from the report. If they are stuck, a hint appears after a few seconds. Use of the hint is recorded.

Navigate with Previous / Next / Unanswered, or the keyboard: T true, F false, ← → move, U jump to the next unanswered. Questions within a topic are randomised, so no two attempts are identical.

Categories & the "All Categories" viewMove between topics; see your spread

The Category bar switches between topics within the subject. All Categories shows progress across the whole subject at a glance, so students can target the topics they've not yet covered.

The Feedback tabMastery overview, with filters

Every concept falls into one of four states: Mastered (2/2), Partial (1/2), Needs Work (0/2), or Not Attempted. Filters let a student show only the partial and needs-work concepts, which indicate where revision is needed.

The Utility tabSave, export, email, back up, reset
  • Save Progress: keeps the current state in this browser.
  • Export Results: downloads progress as a text file.
  • Email to Teacher: opens an email with a results summary ready to send (see §5).
  • Backup and Import: download every subject as a restorable file, or restore from one.
  • Reset Subject: clears responses for the current subject only.
  • Change Examination System: switches to another system. VCE is available now; others are being added.
  • Statistics and How It Works: a summary of progress, and the in-app introduction, which can be reopened at any time.
Light & darkFollows the system, or toggle it

The app follows the device's light or dark setting and can be toggled with the ☾ / ☀ button. The accent colour follows each subject. This guide behaves the same way; use the button in the top right.

4 Where the questions come from

Every question is taken from VCAA's published examiners' reports. Those reports record the errors and misconceptions that cost students marks each year. The app presents that advice as practice that students can repeat.

Open VCAA examination reports ↗
How a report becomes questionsFrom dense PDF to active recall

Examiners' reports contain detailed advice, but they are long and easily skimmed. Each point in a report, such as a noted confusion between two ideas or a feature of high-scoring responses, becomes a concept with two true/false prompts, an explanation, and a study tip. Presenting the advice as paired recall helps students retain it.

Open a subject's report directly

Each link opens that study's VCAA examination reports page in a new tab. Subjects available in the app are marked In app.

6 Why there are no accounts

            
There are no accounts because there is nothing for an account to protect. The app keeps each student's progress on their own device and does not send it anywhere unless the student chooses to. Privacy here follows from where the data is stored, not from a policy.

No server in between. No database. No account. No sign-up. Nothing leaves the device unless the student initiates it.


            
No accounts, no sign-upNothing to create, forget, or breach
                
Students do not register, and neither do you. There are no usernames, passwords, or password resets, and no account database to be compromised.


            
No server, no databaseIt's just a file in the browser
                
ExaminersAdvice is a static web page. There is no back end collecting responses, no analytics, and no third party involved. The only data it retrieves is its own question set, which is the same for every user.


            
Stored on the student's deviceIn this browser's local storage
                
Progress is held in the browser's localStorage, under keys such as examinerAdvice-global and examinerAdvice-<subject>. It stays on that device and in that browser. On a different computer, or after the browser's data is cleared, the app starts fresh. There is no central copy.


            
Sharing is user-initiated & consent-orientedThe student addresses the email themselves
                
"Email to Teacher" opens the student's own email application using a link of the form mailto:?subject=…&body=…, which contains no recipient. The student enters your address and chooses to send. Each share is a separate, deliberate action by the student, and your address is never embedded in the app or stored by it.


            
Privacy by device strategyWhy this sidesteps the usual questions
                
Because the data stays on the student's own device and moves only through the student's own email client, the questions a school usually asks of an online tool have direct answers. Where is the data stored: on the device. How long is it kept: as long as the student keeps it. Who else processes it: no one. How is consent obtained: from the student, each time they send. This generally simplifies IT and privacy review.


            
Common questionsData location, other students, devices, consent
                
    
                    
  • Where is my students' data? On each student's device, in their browser, and nowhere else.
    • 
                    
  • Can students see each other's results? No. Storage is per device and per browser.
    • 
                    
  • What if a student changes computer? They start fresh, or carry progress over with Backup and Import.
    • 
                    
  • Do I need parental consent or a privacy review? Follow your school's policy. Because the app collects no personal data, the review is typically simpler than for an account-based tool.
