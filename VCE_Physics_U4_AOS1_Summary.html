Light as an EM Wave

Light is a transverse electromagnetic wave produced by accelerating charges: a changing electric field produces a changing magnetic field, and vice versa. All EM waves travel at c in a vacuum.

Wave equation c = fλ

Standing Waves

A standing wave forms when a travelling wave superposes with its own reflection. Fixed nodes (zero amplitude) and antinodes (max amplitude). For a string/pipe with nodes at both ends:

Standing wave (nodes both ends) λn = 2L/n    n = 1, 2, 3…
The fundamental (n=1) has one antinode and λ = 2L. Only whole numbers of half-wavelengths fit → only certain resonant frequencies are possible.

Diffraction

Waves spread when they pass through a gap or around an obstacle. The amount of spreading depends on the ratio of wavelength to gap/obstacle width.

Diffraction extent spreading ∝ λ/w
Large λ/w (long wavelength, narrow gap) → strong diffraction. Small λ/w → little spreading. This limits imaging resolution: detail smaller than ~λ cannot be resolved (use shorter λ for finer detail).

Young's Double-Slit Experiment

Coherent light through two slits produces a pattern of bright and dark fringes on a screen — strong evidence for the wave nature of light (interference).

Interference conditions (path difference) Constructive: pd = nλ
Destructive: pd = (n + ½)λ n = 0, 1, 2, …
Fringe spacing (L ≫ d) Δx = λL/d L slit–screen distance, d slit separation
Bright fringes = waves arrive in phase (path difference = whole number of wavelengths). Wider spacing Δx from longer λ, larger L, or smaller slit separation d.

Light as a Particle — Photons

Light energy is delivered in discrete packets (photons), each with energy set by frequency.

Photon energy E = hf = hc/λ
Green light, λ = 510 nm: E = hc/λ = (6.63×10−34×3.0×108)/(510×10−9) = 3.9×10−19 J = 2.4 eV.

The Photoelectric Effect

Light above a threshold frequency ejects electrons from a metal surface — evidence for the particle nature of light.

Max photoelectron energy Ek max = hf − φ φ = work function (min energy to release an electron)
One photon ejects one electron. Below the threshold frequency f0 = φ/h, NO electrons are emitted no matter how intense the light. Above it, emission is instantaneous.

The WAVE model fails here: it predicts emission at any frequency given enough intensity/time, and energy building up gradually. Experiment shows a sharp threshold frequency and instant emission — only the photon model explains this.

Matter as Waves — de Broglie

Particles of matter also show wave behaviour. A moving particle has a wavelength set by its momentum.

de Broglie wavelength & momentum λ = h/p    p = h/λ
Electron diffraction (rings like X-ray patterns) is direct evidence for the wave nature of matter. A photon and an electron of the SAME wavelength have the SAME momentum (p = h/λ).
Electron at v = 1.0×107 m s−1: p = mv = 9.1×10−31×1.0×107 = 9.1×10−24; λ = h/p = 6.63×10−34/9.1×10−24 = 7.3×10−11 m.
Photon momentum at λ = 5.0×10−7 m: p = h/λ = 6.63×10−34/5.0×10−7 = 1.3×10−27 kg m s−1. An electron with the same λ has the same p.

Quantisation & Atomic Spectra

Electrons in atoms occupy discrete energy levels. A photon is absorbed or emitted only when an electron jumps between levels, with photon energy equal to the energy gap.

Photon energy = level difference ΔE = hf = hc/λ
SpectrumHow it forms
Emission (bright lines)Electrons drop down levels, emit photons
Absorption (dark lines)Specific photons absorbed, electrons jump up
Line spectra prove energy levels are quantised: only photons matching an exact gap interact. Each element has a unique "fingerprint" of lines (e.g. metal vapour lamps).
An electron drops between levels 3.0 eV apart: photon energy = 3.0 eV = 3.0 × 1.6×10−19 = 4.8×10−19 J. λ = hc/E = (6.63×10−34×3.0×108)/4.8×10−19 = 4.1×10−7 m (visible).

Dual Nature

The single-photon and single-electron double-slit experiments both build an interference pattern one particle at a time → light AND matter show wave–particle duality.

Special Relativity — Postulates

Classical mechanics fails as speeds approach c. Einstein's two postulates:

  • The laws of physics are the same in all inertial (non-accelerating) frames.
  • The speed of light c is the same for all observers, regardless of the motion of source or observer.
The Michelson–Morley experiment found NO change in the speed of light with Earth's motion (a "null result") — no ether — consistent with the second postulate.

Proper Time, Proper Length & γ

Proper time t0: time between two events measured in the frame where they happen at the same place. Proper length L0: length measured in the frame where the object is at rest.

Lorentz factor & transformations γ = 1/√(1 − v2/c2)
t = γt0   (time dilation)
L = L0/γ   (length contraction)
γ ≥ 1 always. Moving clocks run slow (dilated time is longer); moving objects are shortened along the direction of motion. Effects only matter as v → c. Proper time is the SHORTEST time; proper length is the LONGEST length.
At v = 0.80c, γ = 1/√(1−0.64) = 1/√0.36 = 1.67. A 10 s proper interval is observed as t = 1.67×10 = 16.7 s; a 10 m proper length contracts to L = 10/1.67 = 6.0 m.

Evidence & Applications

  • Muons: reach the ground despite short half-lives — time dilation (our frame) / length contraction (muon frame) lets them survive the trip.
  • Particle accelerators: must be designed for relativistic lengths and times of fast particles.
  • GPS: satellite clocks are corrected for relativistic effects to keep positioning accurate.

Mass–Energy Equivalence

Rest, total & kinetic energy E0 = mc2   (rest energy)
Etot = γmc2 = Ek + E0
Ek = (γ − 1)mc2
Mass and energy are interchangeable. Applies to energy released by mass conversion in the Sun, electron–positron annihilation, and nuclear transformations in accelerators.
Rest energy of an electron (m = 9.1×10−31 kg): E0 = mc² = 9.1×10−31×(3.0×108)² = 8.2×10−14 J ≈ 0.51 MeV. Electron–positron annihilation releases two such photons.
