    What is real, what is simplified

    This is a teaching model. The growth is honest in shape and order of magnitude, but several numbers are defaults, not measured constants for this dish.

    The plate is a biased subset, not a census

    Standard media classically recover near 1 percent of the cells actually present. Many environmental cells are viable but do not grow on this agar, so the dish under counts and over represents the easy growers.

    Room temperature, never 37 degrees

    Incubation here runs from 15 to 30 degrees. A 37 degree incubator would select for organisms that grow at body temperature, including potential human pathogens, so schools avoid it. That is why 37 is marked off on the temperature control.

    Radial rate is an order of magnitude default

    Colony radius grows at roughly 1 mm per day for bacteria. That figure is rounded from lab strains on defined agar. Mixed flora at room temperature are not well quantified, so treat it as a defensible default.

    The spreading mechanism is still being researched

    The model uses the observed fact that radius grows in a straight line over time. Whether that is driven by nutrient diffusion or by mechanical verticalisation of interior cells is an open question. Only the phenomenon is simulated.

    What-if temperature

    In what-if mode the slider reshapes the whole week from a single constant temperature. A real incubator's past would not change retroactively, so this is a thought experiment, not a record.

    Counting

    One colony grows from one founding cell, which is the basis of colony forming unit counting. A colony becomes a naked eye dot only once it holds millions of cells, so nothing is visible for about the first day even though division is well underway.

    About

    Swab and Culture, version 1.0.0. A deterministic, closed form simulation. State is a maths function of elapsed time, a fixed seed, and the per colony parameters, so closing the tab and returning later shows real progress.

    Reading the dish honestly

    Nothing is visible for about the first day. Colonies become naked eye dots near 24 to 48 hours, reach final size over a few days, and moulds appear later and overgrow. Use the integrity panel for what is real and what is simplified.

    Population over time

    A bacterial growth curve: lag, rapid growth, a plateau, then a death phase as nutrient runs out and waste builds up. The lines fill in up to the current time, and the dashed line marks now. Counts are a model, not a measurement.

    Swab a surface

    Pick where the swab came from. A dirtier, higher touch surface transfers more colony forming units, so more colonies will appear.

    23 °C, room temperature. Schools stay at or below 30 and never use 37, which would favour pathogens. Warmer means faster growth.
