Circular Motion — 1B Overview

Key Concepts

  • Uniform circular motion: constant speed, changing velocity direction
  • Centripetal force: always points to circle center
  • Velocity: always tangent to the circle
  • Common scenarios: cars on curves, vertical loops, banked tracks, objects on strings

Core Formulas

  • ac = v²÷r
  • Fc = m·v²÷r
  • Banked track: v = √(r·g·tan θ)
  • Top of loop: Fc = T + m·g
  • Bottom of loop: Fc = T − m·g
Critical insight: Centripetal force is NOT a new force—it's the net result of other forces (tension, gravity, normal, friction) pointing toward the center.

Horizontal Circular Motion

  • Uniform circular motion: Object moves in circle at constant speed
  • Speed is constant but velocity changes (direction changes)
  • Acceleration needed → net force needed → toward center
Car turning corner: Friction provides centripetal force
Satellite orbiting: Gravity provides centripetal force

Example: 1000 kg car, v = 32.4 km/h, r = 3.56 m

Reveal solution
  • Convert: 32.4 ÷ 3.6 = 9.00 m/s
  • Fc = m·v²÷r = 1000 × 9²÷3.56 = 2.28 × 10⁴ N

Vertical Circular Motion — String/Loop

Object on string swinging vertically has TWO forces: tension + gravity

Top of circle

  • Both T and m·g point to center
  • Fc = T + m·g
  • T = m·v²÷r − m·g
  • Minimum v when T = 0: v = √(r·g)

Bottom of circle

  • T points to center, m·g points away
  • Fc = T − m·g
  • T = m·v²÷r + m·g
  • Maximum tension (string might break!)
Roller coasters use teardrop shapes (smaller r at top) to reduce entry speed needed!

Worked Example — Ball on String

1.27 kg ball, 0.511 m string, vertical circle

Part a: v = 4 m/s, find tension at bottom

Reveal solution
  • T = m·v²÷r + m·g
  • T = (1.27)(4²)÷0.511 + (1.27)(9.8)
  • T = 39.8 + 12.4 = 52.2 N

Part b: Find v for zero tension at top

Reveal solution
  • At top with T = 0: Fc = m·g
  • m·v²÷r = m·g
  • v = √(r·g) = √(0.511 × 9.8) = 2.24 m/s

Vehicle on Bumps and Dips

Over a bump (top)

  • m·g points to center, N points away
  • Fc = m·g − N
  • N = m·g − m·v²÷r
  • Feel "lighter" (N < m·g)
  • Lose contact when N = 0: v = √(r·g)

Through a dip (bottom)

  • N points to center, m·g points away
  • Fc = N − m·g
  • N = m·g + m·v²÷r
  • Feel "heavier" (N > m·g)
  • Extra force on suspension!

Worked Example — 1250 kg Car

Part a: Over 15 m bump at 36 km/h, find Fc

Reveal solution
  • v = 36 ÷ 3.6 = 10 m/s
  • Fc = m·v²÷r = 1250 × 10²÷15 = 8.33 × 10³ N

Part b: Speed for zero normal force

Reveal solution
  • v = √(r·g) = √(15 × 9.8) = 12.1 m/s

Part c: Through 25 m dip at 97.2 km/h, find N

Reveal solution
  • v = 97.2 ÷ 3.6 = 27 m/s
  • N = m·v²÷r + m·g = 1250 × 27²÷25 + 1250 × 9.8
  • N = 36450 + 12250 = 4.30 × 10⁴ N

Banked Tracks

Banking allows turns without relying on friction!

  • Track tilted at angle θ to horizontal
  • Normal force has horizontal component → provides Fc
  • Vertical component of N balances weight
Key formulas:
Fc = m·g·tan θ (no friction needed)
v = √(r·g·tan θ) (ideal banking speed)

Used in: velodromes, NASCAR tracks, highway on/off ramps

Worked Example — 4500 kg Truck

Banked track: θ = 30°, r = 75 m

Part b: Fc at 50.4 km/h

Reveal solution
  • v = 50.4 ÷ 3.6 = 14 m/s
  • Fc = m·v²÷r = 4500 × 14²÷75 = 1.18 × 10⁴ N

Part c: Maximum speed without friction

Reveal solution
  • v = √(r·g·tan θ) = √(75 × 9.8 × tan 30°)
  • v = √(75 × 9.8 × 0.577) = 20.6 m/s

Object on String — Horizontal Circle

Ball attached to fixed point, swings horizontally (conical pendulum)

  • Two forces: tension T (at angle θ) and weight m·g
  • Vertical: T·cos θ = m·g (no vertical motion)
  • Horizontal: T·sin θ = Fc (provides circular motion)
From triangle:
T = m·g ÷ cos θ
Fc = m·g·tan θ

Example: 4.83 kg ball, θ = 32°

Reveal solution
  • T = m·g ÷ cos 32° = 47.3 ÷ 0.848 = 55.8 N
  • Fc = m·g·tan 32° = 47.3 × 0.625 = 29.6 N

Section 1B Questions — Multiple Choice

  1. Ball swings anticlockwise, string breaks at side. Direction?
  2. Car needs F at velocity v. What if velocity doubles?
  3. Truck on 75 m radius at 15 m/s. Acceleration?
Solutions
  1. B - Tangent to circle (perpendicular to radius)
  2. D - F ∝ v², so 2v → 4F
  3. A - a = v²÷r = 15²÷75 = 3 m/s²

More MC Questions

  1. Direction of acceleration for circular motion?
  2. Ball hanging in turning truck, angle θ?
  3. Toy truck on 1.6 m loop, minimum speed at top?
Solutions
  1. C - Toward center of circle
  2. C - tan θ = a÷g = 3÷9.8, θ = 17.0°
  3. C - v = √(r·g) = √(1.6 × 9.8) = 4.0 m/s

Short Answer Problems 7-9

Q7: 950 kg vehicle, valley r = 40 m, v = 79.2 km/h

Solution
  • v = 79.2 ÷ 3.6 = 22 m/s
  • N = m·g + m·v²÷r = 950 × 9.8 + 950 × 22²÷40
  • N = 9310 + 11495 = 2.08 × 10⁴ N

Q8: Banking angle for r = 75 m at 72 km/h

Solution
  • v = 72 ÷ 3.6 = 20 m/s
  • tan θ = v²÷(r·g) = 400÷(75 × 9.8) = 0.544
  • θ = 28.5°

Q9: 3 kg ball, 52.1 cm string vertical circle

Solution
  • a) Zero tension at top: v = √(0.521 × 9.8) = 2.26 m/s
  • b) Max T = 100 N at bottom: 100 = 3v²÷0.521 + 29.4
  • Solving: v = 3.49 m/s

Short Answer Problems 10-12

Q10: 464 g ball, 1.5 m string, 30° to vertical

Solution
  • a) r = 1.5 × sin 30° = 0.75 m
  • b) v = √(r·g·tan 30°) = √(0.75 × 9.8 × 0.577) = 2.06 m/s
  • c) T = m·g÷cos 30° = 4.55÷0.866 = 5.25 N
  • d) Fc = m·g·tan 30° = 4.55 × 0.577 = 2.63 N

Q11: Zero-g flight at 180 m/s, 8000 m altitude

Solution
  • a) For "zero-g": N = 0, so m·v²÷r = m·g
  • r = v²÷g = 180²÷9.8 = 3306 m
  • b) Gravity still acts! "Zero-g" means N = 0 (free fall)

Q12: 1750 kg car, 24° bank at 68.4 km/h

Solution
  • b) v = 19 m/s, r = v²÷(g·tan θ) = 361÷(9.8 × 0.445) = 82.8 m
  • c) Horizontal component of normal force provides centripetal force
