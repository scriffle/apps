1A ‚Äî Properties of Waves

Waves transfer ENERGY without transferring MATTER. The medium vibrates but does not travel with the wave.

Transverse: vibrations ‚ä• to direction of travel ‚Äî light, water waves, all EM waves.

Longitudinal: vibrations ‚à• to direction of travel ‚Äî sound. Has compressions (high density) and rarefactions (low density).

Mechanical waves need a medium. Non-mechanical (EM) waves do not ‚Äî travel through vacuum.

Dispersion: In glass, different Œª travel at slightly different speeds ‚Üí different n ‚Üí different refraction ‚Üí white light separates into colours.

Mirages

Inferior (hot road): Air near hot surface = low n. Light bends away from normal through progressively lower-n layers; near-TIR curves it back upward. Observer traces ray back ‚Üí sees upside-down "puddle" of sky.

Superior (ocean): Cool air near water = higher n. Light from distant object bends downward over horizon through warmer (low-n) air above ‚Üí reaches observer. Distant ship appears to float above horizon.

Both mirages: refraction (and near-TIR) through air layers with different T ‚Üí different n ‚Üí displaced/inverted image.

2A ‚Äî Thermal Energy & Temperature

Thermal energy = SUM of all random KE + PE of ALL molecules in a system. Temperature = measure of average KE. Heat = FLOW of thermal energy from high T to low T.

Higher T ‚Üí higher average molecular KE ‚Üí molecules move/vibrate faster. A sparkler spark (~1000¬∞C) has few molecules ‚Üí high T but low total thermal energy ‚Üí won't burn you.

Conduction rate depends on: material, temperature gradient, thickness, contact area. Metals conduct best (free electrons); non-metals/gases are poor conductors.

Radiation: dark/matt surfaces absorb and emit more effectively than shiny/white surfaces.

Sea breeze (day): Land heats faster than sea ‚Üí air above land warms, less dense, rises ‚Üí cooler sea air flows in to replace it. Land breeze (night): reverse ‚Äî land cools faster, air above sea is now warmer, rises ‚Üí air flows from land to sea.
Evaporative cooling: Molecules in liquid have a spread of KE values. Those at the surface with KE greater than the intermolecular bond energy escape into air ‚Äî they carry away above-average KE. Remaining liquid has lower average KE ‚Üí lower temperature. (sweating, Coolgardie safe, wet towel)

2B ‚Äî Blackbody Radiation

A blackbody is a theoretical perfect absorber (all Œª) AND perfect emitter (all Œª, maximum efficiency). All objects above 0 K emit thermal radiation.

Blackbody spectrum shape depends ONLY on temperature ‚Äî not on material. Two blackbodies at the same T have identical emission curves.

As T increases: (1) peak Œª shifts SHORTER; (2) peak intensity rises; (3) total energy emitted increases steeply. Total energy = area under emission vs wavelength curve.

Greenhouse Effect & Global Warming

Greenhouse gases (GHGs): CO2, H2O vapour, CH4, N2O. Transparent to incoming short-wave solar radiation (visible/near-IR), but absorb outgoing long-wave IR from Earth's surface and re-emit it in all directions including back toward Earth (back radiation).

Atmospheric window (~8‚Äì13 Œºm): IR wavelengths that GHGs absorb poorly ‚Üí escapes directly to space.

Sun (5778 K) ‚Üí peak ~500 nm (visible). Earth (288 K) ‚Üí peak ~10 Œºm (far IR). GHGs block this far-IR re-emission, trapping energy and warming the surface.
